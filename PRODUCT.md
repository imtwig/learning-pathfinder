# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Staff members progressing through professional development pathways (Pre-Schema, Apprenticeship, Levels 1-7), managers reviewing and approving submissions, and administrators configuring the system. Users approach this as learners in a supportive educational environment, regularly checking their progress, submitting work, and receiving feedback as part of their professional growth journey.

## Product Purpose

Learning Pathway tracks staff progression through structured professional development stages with manager oversight and approval workflows. Staff submit proof of completion for foundational requirements, managers review submissions with feedback, and the system maintains a historical record of each person's learning journey. Success means staff clearly understand where they are in their progression, feel supported through each milestone, and managers can efficiently review quality work while maintaining development standards.

## Brand Personality

Supportive, warm, encouraging. The system should feel like a trusted learning companion that celebrates progress and recognizes effort. Not cold or bureaucratic, not overly corporate. This is where professionals come to grow, so the interface should reinforce confidence, acknowledge milestones, and make the pathway forward clear and achievable. Think educational institution meets thoughtful product design.

## Anti-references

**Corporate LMS platforms** (Cornerstone, Workday, SAP SuccessFactors): avoid the cold, impersonal, bureaucratic feel of enterprise training systems that treat learning as compliance checkbox theater.

**Generic SaaS dashboards**: avoid the bland cream-and-blue aesthetic with identical card grids and hero-metric templates common to most B2B tools.

**Overly playful or childish designs**: this is for professionals. No cartoonish elements, excessive gamification with badges and points, or condescending tone. Recognition should be meaningful, not decorative.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity of progression**: staff should always know where they are, what's next, and what's required. No hidden steps or unclear expectations.

2. **Recognize effort, celebrate milestones**: acknowledge completed work and progression in ways that feel meaningful. Small moments of encouragement matter in long learning journeys.

3. **Manager efficiency meets staff transparency**: managers need fast, focused review workflows. Staff need visibility into review status and feedback. Both should feel the system respects their time.

4. **Educational warmth, professional respect**: strike the balance between supportive learning environment and serious professional development. Not corporate-sterile, not school-playful. Thoughtful and human.

5. **Progressive disclosure**: show what's relevant now. Don't overwhelm staff with all seven levels when they're in Pre-Schema. Reveal complexity as they progress.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. Ensure sufficient color contrast (4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components), full keyboard navigation support, semantic HTML structure for screen readers, and meaningful focus indicators. Consider reduced motion preferences for animations. All interactive elements must have accessible labels, and form validation should provide clear, actionable error messages.
